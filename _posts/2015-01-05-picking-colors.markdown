As a designer, how you use color directs the use of a product. Color choices define your personal style, just look at the vibrant hues of <a href="http://www.paul-rand.com/" data-href="http://www.paul-rand.com/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" target="_blank">Paul Rand</a>. Here are a few techniques I use when picking colors.</p><h3 name="1804" id="1804" class="graf--h3 graf-after--p">HSB for days</h3><p name="e877" id="e877" class="graf--p graf-after--h3">I always use a tried and true method for picking colors. I heavily employ the HSB (Hue, Saturation, Brightness) color model to create color schemes. Here is a description from the Illustrator Help docs.</p><blockquote name="2acb" id="2acb" class="graf--blockquote graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--blockquote-strong">Hue -</strong> Color reflected from or transmitted through an object. It is measured as a location on the standard color wheel, expressed as a degree between 0° and 360°. In common use, hue is identified by the name of the color, such as red, orange, or green.</blockquote><blockquote name="92d1" id="92d1" class="graf--blockquote graf-after--blockquote"><strong class="markup--strong markup--blockquote-strong">Saturation -</strong> Strength or purity of the color (sometimes called <em class="markup--em markup--blockquote-em">chroma). </em>Saturation represents the amount of gray in proportion to the hue, measured as a percentage from 0% (gray) to 100% (fully saturated). On the standard color wheel, saturation increases from the center to the edge.</blockquote><blockquote name="de8c" id="de8c" class="graf--blockquote graf-after--blockquote"><strong class="markup--strong markup--blockquote-strong">Brightness -</strong> Relative lightness or darkness of the color, usually measured as a percentage from 0% (black) to 100% (white).</blockquote><figure name="acb9" id="acb9" class="graf--figure graf--layoutInsetLeft graf-after--blockquote"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 195px; max-height: 155px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 79.5%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*kLIA1vNgAun-RBt5wI4KOg.png" data-width="195" data-height="155" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/800/1*kLIA1vNgAun-RBt5wI4KOg.png"></div><figcaption class="imageCaption">HSB color model from <a href="http://helpx.adobe.com/illustrator/using/color.html#rgb_cmyk_hsb_and_lab_color_models" data-href="http://helpx.adobe.com/illustrator/using/color.html#rgb_cmyk_hsb_and_lab_color_models" class="markup--anchor markup--figure-anchor" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">http://helpx.adobe.com/illustrator/using/color.html#rgb_cmyk_hsb_and_lab_color_models</a></figcaption></figure><p name="c532" id="c532" class="graf--p graf-after--figure">When using this model, I lock in the Saturation and Brightness value. Then I play around with the Hue value to find colors that complement each other well. From there, I may create more colors by adjusting Brightness to provide darker or lighter variants for more primary colors, accents, and alternative states (hover, focus, etc).</p><p name="568e" id="568e" class="graf--p graf-after--p">I make color schemes in Illustrator with the Color Picker.</p></div><div class="section-inner sectionLayout--outsetColumn"><figure name="178e" id="178e" class="graf--figure graf--layoutOutsetCenter graf-after--p"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 1020px; max-height: 514px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 50.4%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*hmUANAh5t83Ir0HOeQtUDw.png" data-width="2570" data-height="1294" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/2000/1*hmUANAh5t83Ir0HOeQtUDw.png"></div><figcaption class="imageCaption">We start out with this blue. HSB(200,80,100)</figcaption></figure><figure name="ba19" id="ba19" class="graf--figure graf--layoutOutsetCenter graf-after--figure"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 1020px; max-height: 512px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 50.2%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*yrbTzrbbUgffLwot2SR9RQ.png" data-width="2576" data-height="1294" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/2000/1*yrbTzrbbUgffLwot2SR9RQ.png"></div><figcaption class="imageCaption">We can create an orange to accent it by bringing the hue down to 20°. HSB(20,80,100)</figcaption></figure><figure name="2eb5" id="2eb5" class="graf--figure graf--layoutOutsetCenter graf-after--figure"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 1020px; max-height: 514px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 50.4%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*SANAweCFUrrMdOzYItJT7g.png" data-width="2572" data-height="1296" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/2000/1*SANAweCFUrrMdOzYItJT7g.png"></div><figcaption class="imageCaption">And now for a darker blue, dropping the brightness to 80%. HSB(200,80,80)</figcaption></figure></div><div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn"><h3 name="f012" id="f012" class="graf--h3 graf-after--figure">Workflow</h3><p name="cb22" id="cb22" class="graf--p graf-after--h3">When starting a logo, I always begin with black and white. This will allow me to focus on the content and spatial relationships over details. Otherwise, it’s kind of like when you would burn time picking just the right font for your middle school assignments.</p><p name="fd3a" id="fd3a" class="graf--p graf-after--p">However, I disagree with the notion of blindly wireframing interfaces without color. Color is hugely important in discerning UI elements and establishing contrast, so I always select my colors beforehand and tweak them as I go.</p><p name="a7e2" id="a7e2" class="graf--p graf-after--p">And now a few quick tips.</p><ul class="postList"><li name="6392" id="6392" class="graf--li graf-after--p">Use global colors as much as you can. This operates similarly to text styles, where altering it later will automatically adjust every instance this color is used. In Illustrator, you can double-click on a swatch to bring up Swatch Options, then check Global.</li></ul><figure name="e6c0" id="e6c0" class="graf--figure graf-after--li"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 700px; max-height: 767px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 109.60000000000001%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*GDdF9IqQWG6163zmFWaCrw.png" data-width="892" data-height="978" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1600/1*GDdF9IqQWG6163zmFWaCrw.png"></div></figure><ul class="postList"><li name="7b2c" id="7b2c" class="graf--li graf-after--figure">Use palettes and libraries. This will help keep your colors consistent between documents.
