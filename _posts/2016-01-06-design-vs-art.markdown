<section data-field="body" class="e-content">
<section name="bf3c" class=" section--body section--first"><div class="section-divider layoutSingleColumn"><hr class="section-divider"></div><div class="section-content"><div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn"><h3 name="3d48" id="3d48" class="graf--h3 graf--leading graf--title">Design vs. Art</h3><h4 name="de14" id="de14" class="graf--h4 graf-after--h3 graf--subtitle">Distinguishing the creative</h4></div><div class="section-inner sectionLayout--fullWidth"><figure name="92df" id="92df" class="graf--figure graf--layoutFillWidth graf-after--h4"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 50%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*-MLkmNbS_W-Rl2VRLflHMw.jpeg" data-width="2000" data-height="1000" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/2000/1*-MLkmNbS_W-Rl2VRLflHMw.jpeg"></div></figure></div><div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn"><p name="1bf6" id="1bf6" class="graf--p graf-after--figure graf--last">This is how I differentiate design and art within the <a href="https://www.google.com/search?q=design+vs+art" data-href="https://www.google.com/search?q=design+vs+art" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" target="_blank">time old discussion</a>.</p></div></div></section><section name="6f2b" class=" section--body"><div class="section-divider layoutSingleColumn"><hr class="section-divider"></div><div class="section-content"><div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn"><h3 name="f5f3" id="f5f3" class="graf--h3 graf--leading">Design is functional. Art is expressive.</h3><p name="1a8b" id="1a8b" class="graf--p graf-after--h3">Design exists to serve a purpose, while art reflects the imagination.</p><figure name="1c48" id="1c48" class="graf--figure graf-after--p"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 490px; max-height: 432px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 88.2%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*vx4g_BGzuV46v26AedPXrA.jpeg" data-width="490" data-height="432" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1600/1*vx4g_BGzuV46v26AedPXrA.jpeg"></div><figcaption class="imageCaption">(<a href="http://artiseverywhere.serraglia.com/design-vs-art/" data-href="http://artiseverywhere.serraglia.com/design-vs-art/" class="markup--anchor markup--figure-anchor" target="_blank">http://artiseverywhere.serraglia.com/design-vs-art</a>)</figcaption></figure><h3 name="e952" id="e952" class="graf--h3 graf-after--figure"><strong class="markup--strong markup--h3-strong">Design is communicative. Art is personal.</strong></h3><p name="fa5e" id="fa5e" class="graf--p graf-after--h3">If a design is not consumed by users, it is useless. Interfaces, posters, and furniture alike elicit targeted behavior from people, while art tends to be driven by a more personal motivation.</p><figure name="ed5f" id="ed5f" class="graf--figure graf--startsWithDoubleQuote graf-after--p"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 700px; max-height: 525px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 75%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*7rSzeXkTl8EJSTexLWscKA.gif" data-width="800" data-height="600" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1600/1*7rSzeXkTl8EJSTexLWscKA.gif"></div><figcaption class="imageCaption"><a href="http://dribbble.com/shots/1430556-iOS-New-Gestures" data-href="http://dribbble.com/shots/1430556-iOS-New-Gestures" class="markup--anchor markup--figure-anchor" target="_blank">“iOS — New Gestures” by Javi Pérez on Dribbble</a></figcaption></figure><h3 name="5bf2" id="5bf2" class="graf--h3 graf-after--figure"><strong class="markup--strong markup--h3-strong">Design is intentional. Art is interpretational.</strong></h3><p name="dd75" id="dd75" class="graf--p graf-after--h3">When someone notes that something is “created that way by design,” they mean that there is a specific goal of said element. Art is usually appreciated by the unique experience it provides its consumer, not by its effectiveness or success against metrics.</p><figure name="02e5" id="02e5" class="graf--figure graf--startsWithDoubleQuote graf-after--p graf--last"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 700px; max-height: 311px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 44.5%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*EXjUA1QsubsKoxxEfUSj_w.jpeg" data-width="1807" data-height="804" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1600/1*EXjUA1QsubsKoxxEfUSj_w.jpeg"></div><figcaption class="imageCaption"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guernica_%28painting%29" data-href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guernica_%28painting%29" class="markup--anchor markup--figure-anchor" target="_blank">“Guernica”</a> by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pablo_Picasso" data-href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pablo_Picasso" class="markup--anchor markup--figure-anchor" title="Pablo Picasso" target="_blank">Pablo Picasso</a></figcaption></figure></div></div></section><section name="30ab" class=" section--body"><div class="section-divider layoutSingleColumn"><hr class="section-divider"></div><div class="section-content"><div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn"><p name="ca74" id="ca74" class="graf--p graf--leading">There are certainly aspects that transcend both fields, such as style, composition, and beauty. The distinction may hide under a veil of creative obscurity, but I think design at its core is considerably different from art.</p><p name="40a6" id="40a6" class="graf--p graf-after--p graf--last">This breakdown of semantics is something that helps me understand and appreciate both design and art.
